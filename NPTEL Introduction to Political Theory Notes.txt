Freedom as Autonomy

- "Act only on that maxim by which you can at the same time will that it should become a universal law" Said by Kant
- Self government is when an individual govern one's own self and self-direct his or her own life without being directed or interfered with or restricted by others.
- True freedom is doing something without being restricted by any external forces.
- Moral law is Categorical imperative

Categorical Imperative
  
- Categorical imperative is accepted universally. 
- It is unconditional, independent.
- Governed by the principles of 
1. Universality				2. Humanity			3. Autonomy

Hypothetical Imperative

- Hypothetical imperative has predetermined goals or ends and is more practical in real world

Isaiah Berlin written in his book "Two Concepts of Liberty"

Negative Liberty

- What one like to do having his liberty is restricted from doing it by OTHERS, that is external interference is the concept of Negative Liberty.
- It is Freedom from others

Positive Liberty

- What one like to do having his liberty is done without disturbing OTHERS, that is external interference is the concept of Positive Liberty.
- It is Freedom to others

Gerald MacCallum proposed "Triadic Relation"
- For him, freedom involves simultaneous presence of three things
a) Agent/person who is free/unfree
b) Constraints, restrictions, barriers that make an agent/person free/unfree
c) What is the agent/person free/unfree to do

- An individual or society can do something only when they are free to do them

Forms of Equality

1. Equality of Welfare					2. Equality of Resources					3. Equality of Capabilities

Complex Equality:

> It is not enough to distribute the resources or goods, rather emphasis should be on the creation of public or social goods

Equity - Treat all providing customized resources based on needs 
Egalitarianism (Justice) - Treats all providing what is right for whom rather than providing equally or based on needs
